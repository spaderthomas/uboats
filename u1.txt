<p><p><a href="/boats/u1.htm"><a href="/boats/u1.htm">U-1</a></a> was reported missing on 21 April 1940 after it failed repeatedly to report its position. In fact, the boat had already been mined and sunk on 6 April in the barrage laid by British destroyers on its exit route on 3 March 1940. This mine field probably accounted for several U-boats on their outbound or return journey.</p>

<p><strong>Discovery of the wreck </strong><br />The wreck of <a href="/boats/u1.htm">U-1</a> was located by divers in June 2007 and her anti-aircraft gun salvaged, confirming the loss in mine barrage <em>Field No. 7</em>. The entire aft section of the boat was blown off indicating a direct mine hit. See more information and photos here: <a href="http://wrakkenmuseum.nl/u-1">http://wrakkenmuseum.nl/u-1</a></p></p><div class="content_box_fates"><p align="left"><strong>Previously recorded fate</strong></p><ul><li>Sunk on 16 April 1940 in the North Sea southwest of Stavanger, Norway, in position 58.18N, 05.47E, by a torpedo from the British submarine <a href="/allies/warships/ship/3412.html">HMS Porpoise</a>.  <em>(Postwar assessment)</em><br><strong>Notes</strong>. But this attack was against U-3, which escaped unharmed. </li></ul>
</div>
</p>

Read more about the <a href="/technical/schnorchel.htm">Schnorchel</a> and see list of <a href="/technical/schnorchel_fitted.htm">fitted boats</a>.</p>


<h3>Men lost from U-boats</h3>
<p>Unlike <a href="/men/men_lost.htm">many other U-boats</a>, which during their service lost men due to accidents and various other causes, U-1 did not suffer any casualties (we know of) until the time of her loss.</p><br>
